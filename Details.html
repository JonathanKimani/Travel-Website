More Details on Destinations

Each destination listed below offers a gateway to the heart of Japan’s culture, beauty, and spirit. On the detail page, you’ll find in-depth information about each location’s history, top attractions, entry prices, opening hours, and seasonal highlights.

kiyomizu-dera temple

20 Reviews

Kiyomizu-dera Temple, one of Kyoto's most iconic landmarks, offers breathtaking views from its famous wooden stage built without nails, perched 13 meters above the hillside. Visitors can explore the Otowa Waterfall for blessings, walk the love stones at Jishu Shrine, admire historic pagodas, and enjoy seasonal beauty like cherry blossoms in spring or vibrant autumn leaves. The temple is open daily from around 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM, with extended evening hours during special illumination events.

¥500

location

Shibuya Crossing

50 Reviews

Shibuya Crossing is Tokyo’s busiest and most iconic intersection, where thousands of people cross in all directions at once. Located outside Shibuya Station by the Hachikō statue, it’s completely free to visit and walk across. For a bird’s-eye view, you can check out paid spots like Shibuya Sky, or head to free decks like Magnet by Shibuya109. It’s a pure Tokyo experience — loud, alive, and legendary.

¥1200

location

OKASA CASTLE

500 Reviews

Osaka Castle is one of Japan’s most iconic historical sites, known for its massive stone walls, gold accents, and panoramic views from the top floor. Originally built in 1583 by Toyotomi Hideyoshi, it now houses a museum that showcases samurai armor, artifacts, and the history of the unification of Japan. The castle is surrounded by a huge public park, cherry blossom trees, and moats that glow during hanami season.

¥600

MOUNT FUJI

250 Reviews

Mount Fuji is Japan’s tallest and most iconic mountain at 3,776 meters, known for its near-perfect cone shape and spiritual significance. It’s a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular spot for hiking, photography, and scenic viewing. Climbing is open from early July to early September, and starting 2025, there's a required ¥4,000 entry fee plus an online reservation due to overcrowding and conservation efforts.

¥40000

location

TAKENAKA INARI SHRINE

50 Reviews

Takenaka Inari Shrine is a charming and free-entry hidden gem perched atop Yoshida-yama in northern Higashiyama, Kyoto, offering a peaceful retreat among a series of vermilion torii gates and seasonal cherry blossoms—especially magical in early April when pale pink petals contrast against the red gates. The serene walk spans roughly 170 meters, leading to the main worship hall and several small shrines tucked amid greenery. Built in its current form around 1840, this tiny sanctuary provides a tranquil alternative to crowded tourist spots and requires only a short 20-minute walk from Jingu Marutamachi Station

Free Entry

TOKYO SKYTREE

50 Reviews

Tokyo Skytree stands at 634 meters tall, making it the tallest structure in Japan and one of the best spots for panoramic city views. Located in the Sumida district, it features two observation decks: Tembo Deck and Tembo Galleria (450 m). Tickets cost depending on the deck and day, with discounts for kids and early bookings.

¥3600

Higashi Chaya District

50 Reviews

igashi Chaya District in Kanazawa is a beautifully preserved Edo-era geisha quarter filled with wooden chaya (tea houses), narrow stone streets, and timeless latticed façades—free to wander and perfect for photography. While most tea houses are exclusive, you can tour places like O‑chaya Shima or Kaikaro, which hosts interactive geisha performances on select dates.
